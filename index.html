Overview

Zhou Lan is an early career biostatistics investigator at the Center for Clinical Investigation (CCI) within the Channing Division of Network Medicine at Brigham and Women's Hospital. Zhou collaborates closely with investigators in the Department of Radiology (0.6 FTE). He earned his Ph.D. in Statistics from North Carolina State University and received the Paige Plagge Award. His research focuses on statistical methodologies for MRI data, spatial statistics, Bayesian computing, and longitudinal data analysis. His work appears in journals including JRSS, JAMIA Open, Biometrics, Bioinformatics, and more.

Research

Statistical Methodologies for Diffusion MRI:

The structural connections within the brain’s white matter are essential for its function. Diffusion MRI tractography allows for the in-vivo reconstruction of white matter fiber pathways. Diffusion MRI poses significant challenges due to their high dimensionality, spatial correlation, and complex signal structures. My research focuses on developing and applying cutting-edge statistical and computational methods to unlock the full potential of such data. The motivating data includes HCP Young Adult - -Connectome, Adolescent Brain Cognitive Development (ABCD) Study, and Alzheimer's Disease Neuroimaging Initiative (ADNI)

  • FMQ Regression: Quantile-based modeling of fiber bundle microstructure from periphery to core.
  • Geostatistical Models: Spatial modeling of diffusion tensors and positive-definite matrices.
  • Imaging Genetics: Extensions linking imaging biomarkers with genetic variation.

Magnetic Resonance Spectroscopy (MRS): I co-developed pipelines for QC in MRS and proposed the Neurometabolic Network (NMetNet) to describe inter-metabolite dependencies.

  • Integrated MRS and fMRI analyses for functional neurological disorder (FND).
  • Applications in brain tumor biomarkers and predictive modeling.

Statistical Theory for Matrix-Valued Data: My statistical theory contributions include:

  • EM-based composite likelihood classification of correlated Wishart matrices.
  • Bayesian variable selection in multi-group scalar-on-vector-valued image regression.

Clinical/Epidemiological Decision Support: I analyze the impact of NLP-derived data quality and use spatial models to support HIV prevention strategies.
